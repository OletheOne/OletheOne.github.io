The Build: Bloodhound Vagabond

Read First

The single most forgiving, efficient way through Elden Ring: a tanky knight wielding Bloodhound's Fang, backed by the Mimic Tear. High damage, built-in bleed, a dodge baked into your weapon skill, and a clone of yourself tanking every boss.

Why this build wins

Bloodhound's Fang is a curved greatsword you can grab within the first hour. It deals huge damage, causes blood loss (a chunk of the enemy's health bar when the meter fills), and its weapon skill — Bloodhound's Finesse — is an attack that doubles as a backflip dodge. It upgrades with Somber Smithing Stones, which means only ten stones total to max it, no farming dozens of regular stones.

The Mimic Tear spirit ash summons a copy of you, with your weapon and gear. From the mid-game onward, every boss fight becomes two-on-one in your favor. Until then, the Lone Wolf Ashes carry you.

Ignore Mind, Intelligence, Faith, and Arcane entirely. If you ever want to experiment, Rennala (Phase IV) lets you fully respec.

How to actually fight

The Golden Rule

Elden Ring's real difficulty setting is preparation. Flask upgrades (Golden Seeds & Sacred Tears), weapon upgrades (Somber Stones), and Vigor matter far more than skill. This guide front-loads all three.

Phase I — Limgrave

Levels 1–20

The opening hours set up your entire run: your mount, your flask, your spirit summons, and — most importantly — your endgame weapon.

Step-by-step

Varré & the Blood Palace (C) — that smug white-masked man at the First Steps grace starts a questline worth remembering: it later unlocks the game's best rune farm. Exhaust his dialogue now; the rest can wait.

Companions of the Road (H) — Limgrave seeds several great NPC stories: free Alexander the jar north of Stormhill, be kind to a talking tree near the Coastal Cave, and chat with the samurai under the ruins by Agheel Lake.

The main event: claim Bloodhound's Fang

Evergaol Duel · Recommended Level 12–18

Bloodhound Knight Darriwil

The Forlorn Hound Evergaol sits on the southern cliffs of Limgrave, just northeast of the Bridge of Sacrifice. Activate the seal to enter. No spirit summons work inside — this one's on you.

  • He's fast but fragile. Stay locked on, hold your shield up, and punish the end of his lunging combos with one or two hits — then back off.
  • His long leaping slash is your biggest opening: dodge toward him and counter.
  • If you struggle, level Vigor a few more times and return — the arena isn't going anywhere.

Reward: Bloodhound's Fang. Equip it the moment you meet Str 18 / Dex 17. This weapon carries you to the credits.

Efficiency Tip · Margit's Shackle

Once you have ~5,000 runes, return to Patches in Murkwater Cave and buy Margit's Shackle. Used in the first phase of the Margit fight, it pins him to the ground for a free combo — and it works twice. Even better: it works again on a certain boss in Leyndell, much later. Best 5,000 runes you'll ever spend.

Phase II — Weeping Peninsula

A 45-Minute Loot Loop

Entirely optional territory — which is exactly why it's the perfect low-risk farm for flask upgrades and levels before Stormveil. Ride the loop, grab everything, leave.

Checkpoint

Leaving the peninsula you should be around level 20–25, with 5–6 flask charges healing at +2 or +3 potency. That turns Margit from a wall into a speed bump.

Phase III — Stormveil Castle

First Legacy Dungeon

Your first true test — and your first Great Rune. With the Fang, the wolves, and the Shackle, both bosses fold.

Castle Gatekeeper · Recommended Level 20–25

Margit, the Fell Omen

  • Before the fog: summon the Lone Wolf Ashes, and use Sorcerer Rogier's gold summon sign near the stairs.
  • Open with Margit's Shackle — he's pinned, free jumping heavies. Use the second charge when he gets back up. That's often a third of his health gone before he swings.
  • He delays his attacks to bait early dodges. Watch the weapon, not his body — dodge when it moves, not when it glows.
  • In phase two, his hammer slam has a long recovery. That's your combo window.

Through the castle

Shardbearer · Recommended Level 25–30

Godrick the Grafted

  • Summon Nepheli (sign by the fog gate) plus your wolves. Three targets, one very confused demigod.
  • Stay behind him and to his left; nearly everything he does swings forward and right.
  • Phase two he grafts a dragon head onto his arm — when he winds up the flame sweep, sprint behind him and attack freely. The fire breath is a damage window for you, not him.

After the fight: take Godrick's Great Rune, then ride east from the castle across the long bridge to the Divine Tower of Limgrave to activate it. Equipped with a Rune Arc, it raises all your stats. Also visit Nepheli at the Roundtable Hold afterward — she gives you the Arsenal Charm (extra equip load).

Phase IV — Liurnia of the Lakes

Levels 30–45

A huge region — but our business here is short: seeds, tears, a key, and the easiest demigod in the game, who happens to hand you unlimited free respecs.

Sweep the lake

Raya Lucaria Academy

Shardbearer · Recommended Level 35–40

Rennala, Queen of the Full Moon

  • Phase one isn't a fight, it's a puzzle: smash the chanting students with golden glows to break her shield, then wail on her. Repeat.
  • Phase two allows spirit summons — bring the wolves. Rush her down between spells; she has very little health and no poise.

Reward: her Great Rune (doesn't count for Leyndell entry, but that's fine — Radahn's will) and, crucially, Rebirth: unlimited stat respecs using Larval Tears. If you ever misallocate levels, Rennala fixes it.

Optional Detour · Ranni

Northwest of Liurnia, at Ranni's Rise, you can begin Ranni's questline — the game's best side story, with a legendary weapon and an alternate ending at the end of it. It's not required, but if you take one side path, take this one — and now is the ideal moment to start Act I: see The Age of Stars (Side Path B). It conveniently walks the same road as our main route for the next two phases.

Phase V — Caelid & the Starscourge

Levels 45–60

Caelid is a rotting hellscape, and we're going in anyway — because killing Radahn unlocks the city where the Mimic Tear sleeps, and Caelid's side loot is absurdly good.

First: flip the festival switch

Shardbearer · Recommended Level 50–60 · Fang +5 or higher

Starscourge Radahn

  • This fight is designed around the gold NPC summon signs scattered across the battlefield. Summon every single one, and re-summon them when they fall — the signs reappear as the fight goes on.
  • Stay on Torrent. Let the summons hold his attention while you ride in, land a jumping heavy, and ride out.
  • At half health he leaves the arena and crashes back down as a meteor. Ride away from the targeting shadow, then resume the routine.
  • You are the distraction manager, not the hero. Patience wins this in two or three attempts.

Reward: Radahn's Great Rune — your second qualifying rune for Leyndell — and a cutscene: a falling star punches a hole in Limgrave. That crater is our next stop.

Site of Grace · Rest & Continue

Phase VI — Nokron, Eternal City

The Mimic Tear Heist

One dungeon, one objective: the single best item in Elden Ring. Everything after this fight alongside a copy of yourself.

How the Mimic Works

It clones whatever you're wearing and holding when summoned. It has no FP cost — it costs a chunk of HP instead, which your flask refills. Summon it at every boss fog for the rest of the run. From here on, when a boss card says "summon," it means the Mimic.

The Age of Stars (B) — running Ranni's quest? The Fingerslayer Blade she wants is in this very district, Night's Sacred Ground, a few rooms from the Mimic Tear chest. Grab both in one trip (Act II of the side path).

Beneath the Capital (E) — Nokron's aqueduct (past the Ancestral Woods) hides the coffin-ride to the Deeproot Depths, behind the Valiant Gargoyles. Note it for later — that side path is best taken after Morgott.

Phase VIII — Leyndell, Royal Capital

Levels 75–90 · Vigor 40+

Two Great Runes open the gates. Inside: golden streets, a dragon on the wall, and a familiar face waiting at the throne.

Gatekeeper · Fight Mounted

Draconic Tree Sentinel

  • Guards the capital's main gate. Stay on Torrent the entire fight — circle him, dart in for single jumping attacks, dart out.
  • Respect the lightning in phase two; keep moving laterally and never linger behind him (the horse kicks).

Through the golden city

Shardbearer · Recommended Level 80+ · Fang +7

Morgott, the Omen King

  • Remember Margit? Same Omen, bigger crown — and Margit's Shackle still works. Two free pin-downs in phase one.
  • Summon Melina's gold sign outside the fog, plus your Mimic. Three-on-one.
  • He's aggressive but light — jumping heavies stagger him into criticals repeatedly. Dodge his cursed-sword rain by sprinting sideways.

After the fight: speak to Melina at the grace — she grants the Rold Medallion, your key to the Mountaintops. Before you leave: this is a great moment to bank flask potency, ghost gloveworts, and any capital loot, because the city changes forever near the end of the game.

Beneath the Capital (E) — now is the window: the sewers below Leyndell, the sealed Frenzied Flame, and Fia's finale in the Deeproot Depths are all best done here, after Morgott and before the Forge of the Giants.

Companions of the Road (H) — same window: Nepheli, Kenneth & Gostoc's Stormveil finale must be wrapped up before the capital burns. Check your loose threads now.

Phase IX — Mountaintops of the Giants

Levels 90–105 · Vigor 50+

The Grand Lift of Rold carries you into the endgame. The Mountaintops are linear — follow the road, loot the chapels, and prepare to fight a mountain.

The Haligtree & Malenia (F) — while you're in the Mountaintops, Castle Sol in the far north holds half of the secret medallion that opens the game's hidden endgame region. Grab it now even if you save the Haligtree itself for after Farum Azula.

Varré & Mohgwyn (C) — under-leveled for the Fire Giant? The Mohgwyn Palace rune farm erases any deficit in ten minutes — and the endgame-tier palace dungeon itself is worth a visit around now.

Guardian of the Forge · Recommended Level 95–105 · Fang +8/+9

Fire Giant

  • Phase one: stay mounted, and attack the glowing shackle on his left ankle — it takes bonus damage. Ride away when he rolls (yes, he rolls; yes, it's terrifying).
  • Phase two (he tears himself apart): dismount, summon the Mimic, and live under his hands and feet. Almost everything dangerous happens far away from his body — the safest place is uncomfortably close.
  • The fireball barrage tracks — sprint perpendicular, don't roll early.

After the fight: rest at the Forge of the Giants grace and speak with Melina. Warning: committing here transports you to Crumbling Farum Azula and permanently transforms Leyndell — several capital-based NPC quests close forever. If any side business matters to you, finish it first. When ready, commit.

Phase X — Crumbling Farum Azula

Levels 105–120 · Fang +9/+10

A city shattered across the sky, patrolled by dragons and beastmen. The final Somber Ancient Dragon stone for your +10 Fang is found here — keep your eyes on side rooms and corpses as you climb.

Duo Gauntlet · Sleep Is Legal

Godskin Duo

  • The most-hated fight in the game — unless you know the secret: both Godskins can be put to sleep. Craft Sleep Pots (Trina's Lilies + the pot recipe from Fevor's Cookbook) or just brute-force it.
  • Brute-force plan: summon the Mimic, and both of you focus the Noble (the fat one) first. Kill him fast, then two-on-one the Apostle.
  • Use the pillars — the Noble's rolling attack shatters on them.

Companions of the Road (H) — if you've been helping Alexander the jar since Limgrave, his final honorable duel awaits in Farum Azula — and his reward, the Shard of Alexander, is the best talisman this build can equip.

Death Incarnate · Recommended Level 110+

Maliketh, the Black Blade

  • Phase one (Beast Clergyman) is simple: stay close, punish his dagger combos, break his poise with jumping heavies.
  • Phase two, Maliketh goes airborne and hits like doom itself. Summon the Mimic before the fog, let it take aggro, and attack only after his aerial strings end — he's very vulnerable on landing.
  • His Destined Death attacks lower your max HP on hit — respect them, chip patiently, and this ends fast: he has one of the smallest health pools of any endgame boss.

After the fight: a portal at the site of grace returns you to the capital of ash. The end of the road is in sight.

Phase XI — The Elden Throne

Final Gauntlet · Vigor 60

Ashen Leyndell. Four fights stand between you and the throne. Final loadout check: Fang +10, Mimic +10, flask maxed, Vigor 60, Radagon's Soreseal traded out if you're feeling fragile.

First Elden Lord · For Real This Time

Godfrey / Hoarah Loux

  • Phase one Godfrey is honest — dodge the axe, punish the stomps' recovery.
  • Phase two he throws the axe away and wrestles you. His grabs cannot be blocked — dodge them late, the wind-ups are long and theatrical.
  • The Mimic shines here: while he suplexes your clone, you get free combos. Poor clone.
The Final Battle · Two Bosses, One Fog Gate

Radagon of the Golden Order → The Elden Beast

  • Radagon staggers beautifully to jumping heavy attacks — he's a pure physical brawl. Dodge into his hammer slams. His teleport-grab telegraphs with a golden ring; sprint away from it.
  • Save your Mimic's health where you can — it carries over into part two. (If it dies, it dies; you can't re-summon between phases.)
  • The Elden Beast swims away constantly — you can summon Torrent in this arena to close distance instead of jogging across the ocean of stars.
  • When it casts Elden Stars (a comet stream from above), sprint directly toward and past the beast — the stars chase your old position.
  • Attack the glowing brand on its body when it surfaces. Patience, chip damage, and hydration.

Then touch the fractured Marika, and choose your ending. If you finished Ranni's questline (Side Path B) or Fia's dream (Side Path E), their alternate endings are available here too — Ranni's via her blue summon sign in the arena. Mend the ring however you see fit, Elden Lord.

Paths Untrodden — The Side Content

Optional · Read As Referenced

Everything worth doing off the golden road, in the order it becomes available. Each Side Path is self-contained — the Route of Grace signposts the ideal moment to take each one, and nothing here is ever required. Come for the loot, stay for the best stories in the game.

Side Path A — Castle Morne

Weeping Peninsula · Levels 15–25

A short castle siege at the peninsula's southern tip — a misbegotten uprising, a father, a daughter, and a letter that was never going to end well. The perfect first taste of a FromSoftware side quest.

Castle Boss · Recommended Level 18–25

Leonine Misbegotten

  • A fast, leaping brute on an open beach — summon the wolves and let them split his attention.
  • All his combos end with a big recovery window. Guard or dodge through the flurry, then land two heavies and reset.
  • He's vulnerable to poise-breaks: jumping heavy attacks stagger him into criticals.

Reward: Grafted Blade Greatsword — a colossal Strength weapon and one of the game's legendary armaments. Not for this build, but a beautiful trophy (and a Game of Thrones homage — a throne of swords, forged into one blade).

Quest Epilogue

Return to Edgar after the fight, then check on Irina at the roadside. FromSoftware quests rarely end happily — this one sets the tone. Edgar's story continues later at the Revenger's Shack in western Liurnia, where his grief has curdled into something you may have to put down.

Side Path B — The Age of Stars

Ranni's Questline · Liurnia Onward

The longest, best, and most consequential questline in Elden Ring: a witch with a doll's body, a plot older than the Erdtree, and an entire alternate ending. It also hands you two legendary weapons and sends you through the game's most breathtaking hidden zones. If you do one side quest, do this one.

Act I — The witch's tower (during Phase IV)

Act II — The hidden treasure of Nokron (during Phase VI)

Act III — The Lake of Rot and the void (any time after)

Hidden Boss · Recommended Level 60–70

Astel, Naturalborn of the Void

  • A cosmic horror at the bottom of the world. Summon the Mimic and stay glued to its head — most of its scariest attacks (laser, meteor shower) target range.
  • When it teleports, sprint — it often reappears above you for a slam, or behind you for a grab with its pincers. The grab is the only real killer; dodge sideways the instant it materializes.
  • Jumping heavies to the face stagger it for massive criticals.

Finale — The Moonlight Altar

Fair Warning

Completing certain steps turns Blaidd hostile at Ranni's Rise afterward — that confrontation is part of the story, and his armor is yours when it ends. Also: choosing Ranni's ending happens at the very end of the game, and only if you use her summon sign instead of the throne. You can still pick any other ending — having this option costs nothing.

Side Path C — Varré & the Blood Palace

Any Act · The Great Rune Farm

The smug white-masked man who called you maidenless at the very first grace has a proposal — and it ends at Mohgwyn Palace, home of an optional demigod and the most famous rune farm in the game.

The proposal

The farm

Palace Approach Ledge-Road

From the first Mohgwyn grace, the ledge outside overlooks a canyon of dormant albinaurics worth huge runes each — and across the ravine roosts a giant bird that, when aggroed with a ranged attack (a throwing dagger works), charges straight off the cliff. Tens of thousands of runes in seconds, repeatable forever. Whenever the Route of Grace says "if under-leveled, go farm" — this is where it means. Ten minutes here erases any level deficit in the game.

Optional Shardbearer · Recommended Level 90+ · Endgame

Mohg, Lord of Blood

  • Save the man himself for the late game — the palace dungeon between you and him is endgame-tier.
  • His signature move, Nihil, drains your health three times at half HP no matter what you do. Counter it: the Purifying Crystal Tear (dropped by an invader at the Second Church of Marika in Altus, part of Yura's quest) neutralizes the ritual entirely when mixed in your Physick.
  • Otherwise: Mimic, aggression, and dodge the blood-flame claw sweeps diagonally forward. His bleed buildup is the real threat — Stanching Boluses help.
  • Rewards: Mohg's Great Rune, a Remembrance, and — for owners of the Shadow of the Erdtree expansion — the cocoon in his arena is the DLC's front door.

Side Path D — Volcano Manor & Rykard

Mt. Gelmir · Altus Era · Levels 60–75

A polite manor of assassins built into a volcano, run by a demigod who ate his own god-devouring serpent. Join the family business, take a few contracts, then betray absolutely everyone. It counts as a Great Rune, too.

Shardbearer · Recommended Level 65–75 · Bring the Big Spear

Rykard, Lord of Blasphemy

  • A gimmick fight — and the gimmick is glorious. On the arena floor lies the Serpent-Hunter, a great spear made for exactly this. Equip it (no stat requirements) and two-hand it.
  • Its sweeping ranged slashes shred the God-Devouring Serpent. Stay mid-range, swing, and dodge the lava lunges.
  • Phase two, Rykard himself rises: keep swinging, sprint away when he plants his sword for the exploding skull-swarm, and finish the job.
  • Rewards: Rykard's Great Rune, a Remembrance (tradeable for the Blasphemous Blade — a top-tier Faith weapon for a future playthrough), and Tanith's grief.

Side Path E — Beneath the Capital

Post-Morgott · Do Before Burning the Erdtree

Under Leyndell's golden streets: a shunning-ground of omens, a sealed madness that can end the world, and — deeper still — the roots of the Erdtree itself, where Fia's story concludes. All of it becomes harder or stranger once the capital turns to ash, so this window (after Morgott, before the Forge of the Giants) is the time.

The Subterranean Shunning-Grounds

Sewer King · Recommended Level 70–80

Mohg, the Omen

  • The same Mohg as the Blood Palace, before his ascension — with none of the blood-flame nonsense. Shackle, Mimic, jumping heavies. Routine.

After the fight: strike the altar behind his throne — it's an illusion, hiding a long descent of jumps and ledges to the Cathedral of the Forsaken's deepest dark.

The Three Fingers · Point of No Return

At the very bottom of that descent, beyond a door that only opens when you are naked of all armor, waits the Frenzied Flame. Touching the Three Fingers grants an ending — and locks you out of every other ending unless you later undo it with a needle found through Millicent's questline and a hidden dragon's arena. Feel free to walk down and look at the door. Do not open it casually. This is the one choice in Elden Ring the game will not forgive on a whim.

Deeproot Depths & Fia's Dream

Dream Boss · Recommended Level 80+

Lichdragon Fortissax

  • A death-lightning dragon in an open void. Fight it like every dragon: live under its chest, hit the legs, sprint out when it flies.
  • The red death-lightning pillars linger — never dodge into the glowing ground.
  • Reward: the Mending Rune of the Death-Prince, unlocking another alternate ending — again, only if you choose it at the throne. Options cost nothing.

Side Path F — The Haligtree & Malenia

Hidden Endgame Region · Levels 110+ · Fang +9/+10

The most-missed region in Elden Ring, hidden behind two medallion halves and a snowstorm — ending at the hardest boss FromSoftware has ever shipped. This is the true final exam. The Route of Grace's build was quietly designed with her in mind: the Fang's bleed is one of the best answers to Malenia in the game.

Assembling the Haligtree Secret Medallion

The Haligtree itself

The Final Exam · Recommended Level 120+ · Fang +10 · Mimic +10

Malenia, Blade of Miquella

  • Two rules define her: she heals with every hit she lands — even on your shield — and at half health of phase one she unleashes Waterfowl Dance, the most lethal combo in the game.
  • Waterfowl survival: when she leaps and hangs in the air — sprint away immediately. Outrun the first two flurries entirely, then dodge into the final spin. If she's point-blank when it starts, your best odds are dodging diagonally back-left through the first flurry. It's a skill check; expect to pay tuition.
  • Everything else favors you: she has low poise (jumping heavies interrupt her), and bleed procs devastate her — the Fang was born for this. Your Mimic's bleed procs count too.
  • Phase two adds scarlet rot — dodge the dive-bomb opener (run, then roll late), keep Preserving Boluses hotbarred, and never greed past two hits.
  • Rewards: Malenia's Great Rune, her Remembrance, and the right to say you did it. She is optional. She is also the point.

Side Path G — Dragonlord Placidusax

Farum Azula Secret · Levels 110+

Before the Erdtree, there was a dragon who was Elden Lord — and he's still waiting at the eye of Farum Azula's storm for a god who never came back. The most cinematic optional fight in the game, hidden behind a leap of faith.

The Old Lord · Recommended Level 110+

Dragonlord Placidusax

  • A two-headed storm dragon fought on a floating slab of rock. Live at his side, by the hind legs — both breath attacks and the twin-head snaps all rake the space in front of him.
  • When he vanishes into the storm, sprint and keep sprinting — he rematerializes with a dive or a lightning lance aimed at where you were. Roll on the thunderclap.
  • The gold laser-beam sweep: run perpendicular, jump the ground-shock. The Mimic gleefully face-tanks the rest.
  • Reward: his Remembrance — and one of the great "how was this optional?" stories to tell.
